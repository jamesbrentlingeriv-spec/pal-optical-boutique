Privacy Policy

Notice of Privacy Practices
Pal Optical
Effective Date: April 5, 2026

This notice describes how medical information about you may be used and disclosed and how you can get access to this information. Please review it carefully.

Our Privacy Commitment

At Pal Optical, we are required by law to maintain the privacy of your Protected Health Information (PHI) and to provide you with this notice of our legal duties and privacy practices. We are committed to protecting the records of your care and treatment.

How We Use and Disclose Your Health Information

For Treatment: We may use your PHI to provide you with vision care and services. For example, we may disclose your information to opticians, technicians, or other personnel who are involved in your care, or to a lab to fulfill your lens prescription.

For Payment: We may use and disclose your PHI so that the treatment and services you receive may be billed to and payment collected from you, an insurance company, or a third party.

For Healthcare Operations: We may use and disclose your PHI for office operations. These uses are necessary to run the practice and ensure that all of our patients receive quality care.

Appointment Reminders: We may use and disclose PHI to contact you as a reminder that you have an appointment or that your eyewear is ready for pickup.

Uses and Disclosures That Require Your Authorization

Other than the purposes listed above, we will not use or disclose your PHI without your written authorization. You may revoke this authorization at any time in writing.

Your Individual Rights

Right to Inspect and Copy: You have the right to inspect and receive a copy of your health records, including your billing records and prescriptions.

Right to Request Restrictions: You have the right to request a restriction or limitation on the PHI we use or disclose for treatment, payment, or healthcare operations. We are not required to agree to your request, but if we do, we will comply unless the information is needed for emergency treatment.

Right to Confidential Communications: You have the right to request that we communicate with you about medical matters in a certain way or at a certain location (e.g., home phone vs. work phone).

Right to Amend: If you feel that the PHI we have about you is incorrect or incomplete, you may ask us to amend the information.

Right to an Accounting of Disclosures: You have the right to request a list of certain disclosures we have made of your PHI for purposes other than treatment, payment, and operations.

Changes to This Notice

We reserve the right to change this notice. We reserve the right to make the revised or changed notice effective for health information we already have about you as well as any information we receive in the future. We will post a copy of the current notice in our facility.

Complaints

If you believe your privacy rights have been violated, you may file a complaint with our office or with the Secretary of the Department of Health and Human Services. You will not be penalized for filing a complaint.

Return & Warranty

At Pal Optical, we strive to provide the highest quality eyewear and service. Please review our policies regarding returns, warranties, and prescription changes below.

Frame Policies

Returns & Exchanges

We want you to love your new look.

Returns: Frames may be returned within 30 days of purchase.

Exchanges: Frames may be exchanged one time within 30 days of purchase.

Note: Please see the "Lens Policy" section regarding custom lenses involved in frame returns or exchanges.

Manufacturer's Warranty

All new frames are covered under a 1-year manufacturer's warranty against manufacturing defects.

This warranty covers structural defects occurring under normal use. It does not cover accidental damage, loss, or theft.

Lens Policies

Refunds

Because prescription lenses are custom-made medical devices tailored specifically to your eyes and your chosen frame, there are no refunds on prescription lenses.

Scratch Protection & Warranty

Standard Lenses: Lenses purchased without premium coatings do not carry a warranty. We cannot remake lenses due to scratches unless a protection package was purchased.

Premium Protection: A scratch warranty applies only if an Anti-Reflective (A/R) or Premium Scratch Coating is purchased.

Progressive Non-Adapt Policy

Adjusting to "no-line" bifocals (progressives) can take time. If you are unable to adapt to your progressive lenses, we will remake them into lined bifocals or single-vision lenses at no charge.

This exchange must be initiated within 90 days of the original dispense date.

Doctor's Prescription Changes

If your doctor makes a correction to your prescription, we will provide a one-time courtesy remake of your lenses.

The new prescription must be presented within 45 days of your original eyewear dispense.

Service & Adjustments

Courtesy Adjustments

We are happy to perform adjustments on glasses, regardless of where they were purchased.

Adjustment Liability

While our opticians are highly skilled, older frames or frames with hidden stress fractures can sometimes break during adjustment.

In the unlikely event that your frame breaks while we are adjusting it, we offer a 20% courtesy discount on the purchase of a new pair of frames.
